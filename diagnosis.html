There is a few ways to tell if your cat is overweight or not, but there is also ways to tell if your cat is underweight, or a healthly weight aswell so today we will be goin over all three.

  • Underweight

    The ribs, spine, and hip bones can be seen from a distance. There is no body fat that can be seen nor felt.

  • Healthy weight

    The ribs can be felt easily. Weight can be seen from a top down view. Abdominal tuck is present.

  • Overweight

    Overweight: The ribs can't be felt from being covered in fat. Large amounts of fat in the neck, chest, spain, and base of tail. Waist and abdominal tick are missing.

Images like these can be found on most cat-nased websites, as they are important for knowing easily if your cat is underweight, overweight, or perfectly fine with no need to worry. If charts like these aren't helpful enough, you can also always ask your vet to weigh your cat after giving them your cats age and breed.
